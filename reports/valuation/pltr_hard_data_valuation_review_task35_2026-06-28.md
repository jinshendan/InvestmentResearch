## Verdict

The task #30 PLTR hard-data sheet is usable for manager synthesis. It correctly separates hard metrics from AIP anecdotes and shows why PLTR deserves a premium: revenue acceleration, U.S. commercial growth, strong RPO/RDV, customer growth, high NDR, GAAP profitability, and unusually high adjusted FCF.

The valuation conclusion must remain cautious. At roughly 37x FY2026 revenue and 67x FY2026 adjusted FCF, PLTR already prices in a long-duration AI software/platform outcome. The next question is not whether AIP is fake; it is whether AIP can keep converting into durable production revenue fast enough to support the current multiple.

## What Supports the Premium

The strongest evidence points are:

- Q1 2026 revenue of $1.633B, +85% YoY.
- U.S. commercial revenue of $595M, +133% YoY.
- U.S. commercial customer count of 615, +42% YoY.
- Net dollar retention of 150%.
- U.S. commercial RDV of $4.92B, +112% YoY.
- Adjusted operating margin of 60%.
- Q1 adjusted FCF of $925M and FY2026 adjusted FCF guide of $4.2B-$4.4B.
- Government business still growing fast, not acting like a mature drag.

This is materially stronger than a normal "AI story" setup. PLTR has evidence of growth, margin, cash flow, and customer expansion at the same time.

## What Makes the Valuation Tight

The valuation already embeds high expectations:

- ~37x FY2026 revenue is far above typical mature SaaS multiples.
- ~67x FY2026 adjusted FCF leaves limited room for deceleration.
- Consensus appears close to company guide, so near-term upside likely requires another guide raise or strong 2027 visibility.
- AIP revenue is not separately disclosed.
- RDV is not revenue and must be tracked for conversion.
- Government contract ceilings are not obligated revenue.
- Adjusted FCF must be bridged to normalized FCF per share after SBC and dilution.

## AIP Evidence Weighting

For valuation, weight AIP evidence in this order:

1. **High weight:** U.S. commercial revenue, customer count, NDR, billings, RPO, RDV, deal counts.
2. **Medium weight:** production customer renewals, scaled deployments, contract expansions, named use cases with renewal/scale proof.
3. **Low weight:** customer quotes, AIPCon anecdotes, marketing examples, partner announcements without revenue linkage.

The hard-data sheet applies this distinction correctly. The final synthesis should avoid assigning direct valuation value to anecdotes unless they tie to contract expansion or production revenue.

## Mispricing Framing

The right question is:

> Is PLTR being valued as a durable AI operating-system company with unusually high growth and FCF, or is the market over-capitalizing early AIP momentum?

Bull case:
- U.S. commercial growth remains above 80%-100%.
- NDR stays >140%.
- AIP moves from pilot to production with measurable expansion.
- Adjusted operating margin and FCF margin remain elite.
- Government contracts convert to durable revenue without crowding out commercial growth.

Bear case:
- U.S. commercial growth decelerates sharply.
- RDV does not convert into revenue.
- AIP remains hard to isolate as a revenue product.
- International commercial remains weak.
- SBC/dilution reduce per-share FCF quality.
- Multiple compresses toward high-growth software peers.

## Review Conclusion

Task #30 is approved for synthesis with caveats.

The hard data supports a premium valuation, but not an unconstrained one. At the current snapshot, PLTR needs sustained exceptional execution to justify the multiple. For a trade memo, the next required work is a verified adjusted-close / consensus / peer EV refresh and an AIP conversion-quality tracker.
