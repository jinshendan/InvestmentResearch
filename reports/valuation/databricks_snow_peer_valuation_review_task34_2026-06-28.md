## Verdict

The task #29 peer sheet is directionally sound and usable for manager synthesis. Its core framing is correct: Databricks at $100B / $134B / $175B should be tested against Snowflake and broader public software comps, but private run-rate revenue and private preferred-share valuation cannot be treated as the same thing as public GAAP revenue and public common-equity market cap.

My valuation view:
- **$100B:** supportable if S-1 quality is strong.
- **$134B:** defensible but demanding; requires clear premium evidence versus SNOW.
- **$175B:** stretch / platform-option case, not base case.

## What Must Be True

### $100B scenario

This is investable/attractive only if:
- Revenue growth remains meaningfully above SNOW, ideally >40%-50%.
- NRR remains around 130%-140% or better.
- Positive FCF is audited-quality, not temporary working-capital timing.
- AI product run-rate remains at least ~25% of revenue run-rate.
- IPO terms do not heavily favor preferred holders over new public common.

### $134B scenario

This requires Databricks to deserve a premium category:
- >50% growth into the filing window.
- NRR around or above 140%.
- AI product run-rate moving from $1.4B toward $2B+.
- Medium-term FCF margin path of 10%-15%+.
- Evidence that AI revenue is high-quality software/platform revenue, not mostly compute/model pass-through.

### $175B scenario

This should not be base case. It needs:
- >55%-60% growth or a clearly incremental AI product growth leg.
- 15%+ medium-term FCF margin path.
- AI attach >35%-40% or credible path to $2.5B-$3B+ AI product run-rate.
- Public-market willingness to value Databricks closer to a platform-option asset than a SNOW peer.

## Main Methodology Caveats

1. **Run-rate is not GAAP revenue.**  
   $5.4B run-rate is useful for screening but must be reconciled to audited GAAP revenue in an S-1.

2. **Private valuation is not public common equity value.**  
   The $134B private valuation may include preferred terms, liquidation preferences, structured rights, and lower liquidity.

3. **Snowflake multiple needs market-data refresh before trading.**  
   SNOW's ~$86B market cap and ~14.7x guide multiple are useful anchors, but any actual trade decision needs updated adjusted close, diluted share count, cash, debt, EV, and consensus.

4. **AI product run-rate quality is central.**  
   The $1.4B AI product run-rate should be decomposed into subscription/software, compute pass-through, services, and consumption economics if possible.

5. **FCF quality matters more than positive/negative label.**  
   Positive FCF LTM is helpful, but IPO analysis needs gross margin, capex intensity, deferred revenue, stock comp, working capital, and normalized FCF margin.

## Recommended Synthesis Language

Databricks should be treated as a high-readiness IPO watch item and a valuation anchor for public data/AI software peers.

- Below $100B: attractive if S-1 confirms growth, margin, and revenue quality.
- $100B-$134B: selective; requires clear superiority to SNOW in growth, NRR, and AI monetization.
- $134B-$175B: cautious; private-market premium leaves limited error margin.
- Above $175B: avoid unless S-1 shows extraordinary AI platform economics.

## Required Before Final Valuation View

- S-1 or audited GAAP revenue.
- Gross margin and FCF bridge.
- Stock-based compensation and dilution.
- Customer concentration and cohort retention.
- Product split: lakehouse/data, AI products, compute/services/pass-through.
- Preferred-share terms and IPO conversion mechanics.
- Updated public comp table with EV, not just market cap.
