## 1. Purpose

This sheet separates hard financial evidence from AIP anecdotes. It is designed to answer one question:

**Does PLTR's hard data support the AIP operating-system thesis, or is the stock mainly pricing a story?**

The answer after this pass: the hard data is unusually strong, but the valuation is still demanding. Q1 2026 shows revenue acceleration, U.S. commercial momentum, high NDR, expanding deal volume, and very high adjusted FCF. The remaining risk is not "is AIP fake"; it is whether AIP converts from early production momentum into repeatable, recurring revenue quickly enough to justify ~37x FY2026 revenue / ~67x FY2026 adjusted FCF.

Valuation use rule: only the first three categories should carry strong valuation weight today:

1. Hard financial/customer-count evidence.
2. Contract/RDV evidence with conversion tracking.
3. Production customer evidence with renewal/scale indicators.

Marketing events, customer quotes, and partner announcements are useful lead indicators, but should not be valued as recurring revenue until tied to production deployment and contract expansion.
